Questions? We've Got Answers

What exactly is ColorKit?
ColorKit is a Chrome extension that adds visual customization to Google Calendar. It lets you color-code events with unlimited colors, shade entire days, and create visual time blocks — all without modifying your actual calendar data.
Do you collect or share my data?
No. ColorKit runs entirely in your browser. Your calendar data never leaves your computer, and we have zero access to your events. All color settings are stored locally on your device.
Is there a free trial?
Yes! You get a full 7-day free trial with access to all features. No credit card required to start. If you decide ColorKit isn't for you, just don't subscribe — no strings attached.
Which browsers and devices are supported?
ColorKit works on Chrome for desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux). We're focused on perfecting the Chrome experience first. Firefox and Edge support may come in the future based on community demand.
What are "time blocks" and how do they work?
Time blocks are visual shading that appears on your calendar to mark off time periods (like "deep work 9-12" or "lunch"). Unlike fake events, they don't clutter your calendar or sync to other devices — they're purely visual overlays that help you see your ideal schedule at a glance.
Can I request new features?
Absolutely! ColorKit is community-driven. Email your feature requests to adam@calendarextension.com or use the Requests page. Popular requests get prioritized on the roadmap.
Is ColorKit affiliated with Google?
No. ColorKit is an independent product built by a solo developer. It's not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Google in any way. Google Calendar is a trademark of Google LLC.
What if I want a refund?
We offer a 7-day money-back guarantee. If ColorKit doesn't work for you, email adam@calendarextension.com within 7 days of purchase and we'll refund you — no questions asked.
